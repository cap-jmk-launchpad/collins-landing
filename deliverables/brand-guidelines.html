Brand identity

Who Collins is, who we serve, and how we stand apart from legacy marketing platforms.

Identity at a glance

  • One-linerBoost your digital marketing and automation for faster business growth.

Mission

We help digital marketers and small business owners streamline online operations and accelerate sustainable revenue without hidden costs or unnecessary complexity. Collins transforms scattered marketing efforts into a cohesive growth engine so teams can focus on strategy, not infrastructure maintenance.

Positioning

The agile, transparent alternative for modern growth teams.

  • Unified ecosystem vs. fragmented tool stacks

  • Financial predictability with transparent pricing

Audience

  • Primary: Digital marketers and SMB owners deploying SEO, paid ads, and automation quickly.
  • Secondary: Growth teams needing behavioral tracking beyond basic search visibility.
  • Not for: Massive enterprises requiring heavy custom development or niche-only SEO toolkits.

Core values

  1. Transparency — honest pricing and clear capability boundaries.
  2. Agility — rapid deployment without slow support queues.
  3. Empowerment — automation that replaces spreadsheet chaos.
  4. Clarity — features that drive conversion, not feature licensing theater.

Voice & tone

Professional, trustworthy, and evidence-led — the Sage voice with Creator momentum.

How Collins speaks

Every touchpoint should feel like a knowledgeable partner: calm, direct, and grounded in measurable outcomes.

Voice attributes

  • Clarity — translate capability into business outcomes, not infrastructure jargon.
  • Transparency — back claims with specific logic; never overpromise unused features.
  • Pragmatic wisdom — guide real-world constraints before suggesting full overhauls.
  • Growth-driven confidence — encouraging, but always evidence-based.
  • Direct accountability — own limits honestly; offer resolution paths immediately.

Tone by context

  • Marketing — energetic yet professional; trust signals over buzzwords.
  • Product UI — functional and calm; short directive sentences.
  • Documentation — instructive; steps first, theory second.
  • Errors & limits — honest, supportive, with clear workarounds.

Use vocabulary

streamlineautomateintegrate transparentconversionanalytics actionableoptimizedeploy

Avoid vocabulary

bloatlegacysiloed revolutionarysynergygame-changing hidden feesenterprise overhead

Copy examples

Don’t: “Our platform supports seamless integrations between any third-party tool.”

Do: “We connect your SEO content directly to paid ads so every click counts toward revenue — without a 10-hour setup spreadsheet.”

Don’t: “Enterprise-grade value at SMB cost structures.”

Do: “You get behavioral tracking and CRM syncs; pay for usage you drive, not unused seats.”

Neon green for agency surfaces; structured tokens for product UI. Accent discipline in both.

Color rules

  • Reserve neon green or product blue for primary actions and key data — never as decorative fill across entire layouts.
  • Pair accent colors with validated foreground tokens (#040806 on neon, #fafafa on product blue).
  • Use muted surfaces for grouping; keep accent intensity for conversion paths and interactive states only.
  • Maintain WCAG AA contrast for all body text in both light and dark themes.

Typography rules

  • One display family + one body family — no decorative alternates.
  • Use weight and spacing for hierarchy before introducing extra colors.
  • Limit line length to ~65–70 characters for long-form reading.
  • Preload web fonts for marketing; provide system-ui fallbacks everywhere.

Logo usage

The Collins mark pairs neon green energy with clean white geometry. Protect recognition through consistent placement.

Logo system

Primary asset: assets/logo.svg — vector mark with neon green accent stroke and white wordform paths.

Do

  • Maintain clear space equal to half the logo height on all sides.
  • Use the approved SVG on dark marketing backgrounds and neutral light surfaces.
  • Scale proportionally — minimum ~48px digital, ~12mm print.
  • Preserve the neon green accent; it signals brand energy on the mark.

Don’t

  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or add drop shadows to the mark.
  • Substitute unapproved colors outside the token system.
  • Place the logo on busy imagery without a contrast backing plate.
  • Recreate the wordform with alternate type — use the official asset only.

Do's & don'ts

Quick compliance guardrails for copy, visual design, and product deliverables.

Brand guardrails

Copy

Do

  • Lead with outcomes — revenue, time saved, conversion lift.
  • State pricing and limits plainly; invite scrutiny.
  • Tailor messages to SEO → ads → CRM journey stages.

Don't

  • Use hype words (“revolutionary”, “disruptive”) without proof.
  • Hide costs behind “contact sales” for core tiers.
  • Overpromise integrations the unified system cannot deliver today.

Visual

Do

  • Apply Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter consistently.
  • Use accent colors sparingly for CTAs and key metrics.
  • Treat whitespace as an active layout element.

Don't

  • Fill dashboards with decorative color noise.
  • Hard-code hex values — reference design tokens.
  • Use placeholder lorem in customer-facing demos.

Product

Do

  • Map UI to CSS variables from design.md.
  • Ship light and dark themes with parallel structure.
  • Document integration paths for non-technical founders.

Don't

  • Introduce one-off styles outside the token contract.
  • Bury connection logic deep in help centers.
  • Ship components that fail contrast in either theme.

Handoff & checklist

Final QA before publishing collateral, landing pages, or agent-generated assets.

Implementation handoff

Read brand.md for strategy and voice, then design.md for tokens and components. Regenerate this PDF with npm run brand:pdf.

Brand alignment checklist

  • Copy reflects professional, trustworthy Sage tone — no hollow superlatives.
  • One-liner and CTAs explain benefit, not generic “Learn more”.
  • Typography uses Plus Jakarta Sans (headings) and Inter (body) only.
  • Colors map to approved tokens; accent used with discipline.
  • Logo asset unchanged; clear space and contrast verified.
  • Light and dark UI both meet accessibility contrast requirements.
  • Claims align with actual CRM, analytics, and automation capabilities.
