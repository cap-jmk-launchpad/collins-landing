## 1. Brand identity

- **Name:** Collins
- **Tagline / one-liner:** Boost your digital marketing and automation for faster business growth.
- **Category / niche:** Senior-led digital marketing agency for growth brands: strategy, content, paid media, automation, and analytics as one accountable team.

### Mission

Collins helps founders and marketing leads unify SEO, content, paid media, and automation without the overhead of a global agency or the ramp time of building an in-house team. We replace fragmented freelancers and opaque vendor stacks with transparent retainers, senior strategists on every call, and a full-funnel crew that shares one growth plan.

### Positioning

**Studio polish, solo speed for brands that outgrew freelancers but refuse enterprise bloat.**

- **One team, full funnel:** Strategy, content, paid media, CRM, and CRO under one roof. Not siloed specialists who never talk to each other.
- **Senior access by default:** Strategists and operators who ship, not a rotating cast of juniors behind a pitch deck.
- **Transparent partnerships:** Upfront scopes, line-item clarity, and no surprise change orders or nickel-and-dime connectivity fees.
- **Speed without theater:** Strategy sprints kick off in weeks, not multi-month discovery phases.
- **Neon clarity over noise:** Direct communication, measurable outcomes, and reporting that ties to revenue. Not vanity charts.

### Audience

- **Primary:** Founders and marketing leads at B2B and DTC growth brands ($1M–$50M revenue) with active paid and content programs ready to unify.
- **Secondary:** CMOs and growth teams replacing multiple vendors or evaluating agency vs. in-house build-out.
- **Not for:** Enterprise procurement cycles requiring extensive customization, brands seeking cheapest hourly labor, or teams that want a self-serve software platform instead of a partnership.

### Core values

1. **Clarity:** Scopes, timelines, and reporting stay legible. No hidden fees, no jargon walls, no slide-deck theater.
2. **Accountability:** One team owns the full funnel. We measure pipeline impact, not activity counts.
3. **Senior craft:** Experienced strategists and operators on every engagement. Quality over headcount.
4. **Speed:** Discovery within a week, sprints within 2–3 weeks of signing. Momentum is a deliverable.
5. **Partnership:** We integrate with your stack and team. Collins extends your crew; we do not replace your judgment.

## 2. Brand archetype and narrative

### Archetype: Sage (85%) / Creator (15%)

Collins leads with **Sage:** calm authority, evidence-based guidance, and transparent reasoning. We explain the *why* behind channel mix, creative direction, and automation choices. **Creator** shows up in how we build editorial systems, campaign frameworks, and demo experiences that feel crafted, not templated.

### Story type

**Transformation.** Brands move from fragmented vendors and manual spreadsheet chaos to a unified growth engine with one accountable partner.

**Before:** Three freelancers, a legacy agency retainer, and disconnected ad and CRM reporting. Budget conversations are painful. CAC creeps up despite flat spend.

**After:** One senior-led team ships strategy, content, paid media, and automation from a shared playbook. Transparent scopes make board conversations easy. Pipeline gets cleaner quarter over quarter.

**Collins's role:** The embedded growth partner: senior strategists, creators, and analysts who unify channels without enterprise overhead.

### Brand story

Collins is a digital marketing agency for brands that want clarity, speed, and accountable growth partnerships. We serve marketing leads who need SEO, content, paid media, and automation working as one system. Not another bloated retainer or opaque vendor stack.

Where global shops bury clients in junior rotations and six-month discovery phases, Collins delivers senior access, transparent scopes, and sprints that start in weeks. Where fragmented freelancers silo channels and reporting, we connect search intent to conversion, align paid campaigns with content, and automate nurture through operators who understand the full funnel.

Our visual identity (dark surfaces, neon green accents, and confident whitespace) mirrors the brand promise: **neon clarity** in strategy, execution, and partnership terms.

## 3. Voice and tone

### Voice attributes

- **Direct:** Say what we do and what it costs. *Example:* "Retainers are quoted upfront with line-item clarity", not "flexible engagement models."
- **Operator-led:** Speak like practitioners, not pitch decks. *Example:* "We map your next 90 days: channels, quick wins, and scope", not "holistic growth orchestration."
- **Evidence-based:** Claims tie to outcomes. *Example:* "CAC dropped 22% in two quarters", not "transformed their marketing."
- **Warm but efficient:** Approachable without filler. Respect the reader's time.
- **Anti-bloat:** Call out complexity, hidden fees, and vanity metrics when contrasting with industry defaults.

### Tone by context

- **Marketing / landing page:** Confident, specific, founder-direct. Lead with one promise and one proof point per section.
- **Proposals & scopes:** Plain language, line items, timelines. No open-ended "hours buckets."
- **Demo & hyperframes captions:** Short beats: step title, one-line caption, forward motion.
- **Email & contact:** Helpful, fast reply energy. "Tell us where momentum is stuck."

### Vocabulary

**Use:** strategy call, retainer, sprint, full-funnel, senior-led, transparent scope, pipeline, CAC, conversion, nurture, playbook, partnership, ship, clarity, accountable, unify, automate, amplify.

**Avoid:** platform, free trial, sign up, dashboard login, enterprise-grade, synergy, revolutionary, cutting-edge orchestration, leverage (as verb filler), ecosystem (as empty noun).

### Copy examples

- **Don't:** Start your free trial and explore our all-in-one platform. **Do:** Book a 30-minute strategy call. We will map your next 90 days.
- **Don't:** Leverage our holistic marketing ecosystem. **Do:** One team owns SEO, paid media, and automation, with reporting that ties to revenue.
- **Don't:** Contact sales for pricing. **Do:** Retainers and project scopes are quoted upfront with line-item clarity.

## 4. Visual identity (summary)

**Full visual system:** [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md)

### Visual theme

Collins reads as a **neon green agency** on dark surfaces, confident, modern, and operator-focused. The aesthetic is not SaaS-minimal or corporate blue; it is a growth studio with electric accents, generous darkness, and intentional glow.

Accent discipline matters: neon green (`#39ff14`), teal (`#00ff88`), and lime (`#b6ff00`) signal action, emphasis, and gradient highlights. Never decorative noise across entire layouts. Whitespace (dark space) lets stats, testimonials, and hyperframe captions breathe. Typography carries hierarchy; color carries intent.

**Motion** follows the same restraint: beat-synced hyperframe crossfades and scroll reveals confirm progress without competing with CTAs. Neon glow may pulse on the demo stage and active carousel dots only. Never on primary buttons. See [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) §7 for tokens, keyframes, and reduced-motion rules.

### Color palette

- **Background Deep:** `#040806`. Page canvas, primary dark base.
- **Background Surface:** `#0a100c`. Header, elevated panels.
- **Background Muted:** `#0f1610`. Alternating sections, FAQ and demo bands.
- **Background Elevated:** `#141c14`. Cards, feature tiles, testimonials.
- **Primary Text:** `#f0f7eb`. Headlines and body on dark surfaces.
- **Secondary Text:** `#9aab94`. Nav links, supporting labels.
- **Text Muted:** `#6b7a66`. Footer, metadata, helper copy.
- **Neon Green (Accent):** `#39ff14`. Primary CTA, eyebrows, key highlights.
- **Neon Teal:** `#00ff88`. Gradient partner, secondary accent beats.
- **Neon Lime:** `#b6ff00`. Feature icon variants, gradient stops.
- **Accent On:** `#040806`. Text on filled neon buttons.
- **Border:** `rgba(57, 255, 20, 0.12)`. Subtle structure on dark UI.

### App tokens

The live site is **dark-first:** all semantic roles map to CSS variables in `styles.css` (see [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) §2). Do not reintroduce legacy light-theme blues (`#0060b9`, `#485668`) unless building a separate print/PDF export surface.

### Motion (summary)

Collins motion is **restrained neon:** purposeful, not decorative. Hyperframe beats crossfade posters and stagger subtitle captions in sync; section content reveals on scroll; stat numbers count up once on load. Primary CTAs never loop or pulse; glow animation is reserved for the demo theater and carousel progress dots. All motion honors `prefers-reduced-motion` with instant states. Full spec: [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) §7.

### Typography

- **Headings / Display:** Plus Jakarta Sans for hero headlines, section titles, buttons, stats, hyperframe titles.
- **Body / UI:** Inter for lede copy, FAQ answers, form-adjacent text, footer.
- **Spec / code:** Monospace for token names, file paths, agent handoff snippets.

### Logo

Use `assets/logo.svg` in lockup with the wordmark "Collins." Minimum clear space: half the mark height. On dark backgrounds, preserve the neon drop-shadow treatment. Do not stretch, recolor outside the token system, or place on busy photography without a scrim.

## 5. Design principles

1. **Outcome over ornament:** Every section should help a prospect decide: services fit, proof trust, CTA next step. No feature grids for imaginary product modules.
2. **Accent discipline:** Neon accents on CTAs, eyebrows, active nav, and gradient text. Not paragraph body or full-width backgrounds.
3. **Tokens are the contract:** Colors, spacing, motion, and radius come from `styles.css` variables. See [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) for the full map.
4. **Dark-first fidelity:** Design for `#040806` canvas first. Glow and border tokens assume dark surroundings.
5. **Whitespace confidence:** Let hero stats, testimonials, and demo captions breathe. Density signals vendor bloat; Collins signals control.
6. **Show the work:** Hyperframes demo and case-study quotes are first-class proof. Prefer real captions and client outcomes over stock abstractions.
7. **Agent-transmissible:** Brand voice in this file; visual execution in DESIGN.md. Agents read BRAND → DESIGN → `index.html` / `styles.css`.

## 6. Layout principles

- **Section spacing:** Major sections separated by `xl` (40px) to `2xl` (56px) vertical rhythm; internal card grids use `lg` (24px) gaps.
- **Content max width:** `--max-width: 1180px` centered grid; text-heavy blocks (lede, FAQ answers) stay within ~65–70ch for readability.
- **Hero structure:** Eyebrow → H1 (with optional gradient accent span) → lede → primary + secondary CTA → social proof stats. Hyperframe theater occupies the right column on desktop.
- **Grouping / muted backgrounds:** `.panel-muted` (`--bg-muted`) alternates section bands; cards use `--bg-elevated` with subtle borders.
- **CTA prominence:** One primary neon gradient button per cluster ("Book a strategy call"); ghost button for secondary ("Watch the demo", "Email the team").
- **Navigation:** Sticky header, text nav anchors, compact header CTA. No mega-menus.
- **Footer:** Four-column grid: brand blurb, Agency links, Company, Legal. Dense but breathable.

Layout expresses **professional trust through restraint:** aligned grids, predictable section rhythm, and CTAs that stay visible without shouting.

## 8. Do's and don'ts

### Copy

- **Do:** Lead with one concrete claim and one proof point per section. **Don't:** Stack abstract superlatives without operational evidence.
- **Do:** Use "Book a strategy call" as the primary conversion label. **Don't:** Use product signup language ("Start trial," "Create account").
- **Do:** Name services exactly as listed in §7. **Don't:** Invent software features or platform modules Collins does not sell.
- **Do:** Quote transparent pricing philosophy ("line-item clarity," "no surprise change orders"). **Don't:** Imply opaque "contact sales" or hidden connectivity fees.
- **Do:** Attribute testimonials to role + company. **Don't:** Use anonymous "happy customer" filler.

### Visual

- **Do:** Apply neon accent tokens from [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) for CTAs, eyebrows, and gradient text. **Don't:** Revert to legacy blue `#0060b9` or slate `#485668` on marketing surfaces.
- **Do:** Use Plus Jakarta Sans for headings and Inter for body. **Don't:** Introduce decorative serif or display fonts.
- **Do:** Keep dark backgrounds and subtle green borders. **Don't:** Ship light-mode-white SaaS layouts for the public site.
- **Do:** Preserve logo clear space and SVG glow. **Don't:** Flatten the mark onto low-contrast busy imagery.
- **Do:** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. instant states replace scroll reveals and hyperframe animations.

### Agency / handoff

- **Do:** Read [DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md) before changing `styles.css` or component markup. **Don't:** Hardcode hex values that duplicate tokens.
- **Do:** Update hyperframes manifest captions when landing copy changes. **Don't:** Leave demo beats describing a software product.
- **Do:** Keep `docs/`, root, and `deliverables/` brand/design pairs in sync. **Don't:** Fork divergent palettes across copies.

## 10. Document map (agent handoff pair)

Agents and implementers should read in this order:

1. **BRAND.md** (this file): Identity, voice, agency positioning, CTAs, messaging anchors.
2. **[DESIGN.md](./DESIGN.md):** Tokens, components, motion, hyperframes, implementation map to `styles.css`.
3. **`index.html`:** Canonical public copy and section structure.
4. **`styles.css`:** Runtime token definitions and component styles.
5. **`deliverables/brand-guidelines.pdf`:** Human-readable export; strategy summary for stakeholders.
6. **`assets/hyperframes-manifest.json`:** Demo beat titles, captions, posters, segments.

**BRAND.md** defines *why* and *what to say*. **DESIGN.md** defines *how it looks and moves*. Keep both updated together when positioning or tokens change.
