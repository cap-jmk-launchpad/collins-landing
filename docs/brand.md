## 3. Voice and tone

### Voice attributes
- **Clarity** — We cut through technical jargon to present complex data as actionable simplicity; *Example: Instead of listing raw log entries, we summarize trends in plain English so non-tech marketers understand the impact immediately.*
- **Transparency** — Our honesty about capabilities and pricing builds trust before a contract is signed; *Example: We openly state what automation workflows require human oversight versus those that run fully autonomously, avoiding hidden upsells later.*
- **Empowerment** — We shift from teaching users how to manage tools to inspiring them to lead growth strategies; *Example: Copy focuses on "you unlock" rather than "click here to enable," placing the user in control of their business outcomes.*
- **Precision** — Every word serves a specific purpose, reflecting our founder-led philosophy that respects time and budgets; *Example: We avoid flowery adjectives in favor of exact metrics that prove ROI for conversion rate optimization campaigns.*

### Tone by context
*   **Marketing**: Inspiring yet grounded. We speak to the ambition of growing businesses without making empty promises, focusing on tangible results like faster deployment and lower total cost of ownership.
*   **Product UI**: Concise and directive. Interface text gets straight to the point with active verbs that guide the user through automation setup efficiently.
*   **Docs**: Educational and reassuring. Documentation anticipates friction points in complex integrations (like CRM syncing) and offers clear, step-by-step solutions without overwhelming technical density.
*   **Error/limits**: Empathetic but solution-oriented. When systems hit a wall or an API rate limit is reached, we acknowledge the disruption immediately while providing specific steps to resolve it quickly.

### Vocabulary
**Use:** Action verbs (streamline, amplify, integrate), outcome-focused terms (ROI, conversion, velocity), transparency markers (clear pricing, real-time data, direct connection).
**Avoid:** Hype words ("revolutionary," "game-changing"), corporate fluff ("synergy," "ecosystem" used as filler), vague qualifiers ("maybe," "potentially").

### Copy examples
- **Don't**: Experience our revolutionary ecosystem that changes everything. — **Do**: Deploy your first automated lead-nurture workflow in minutes, not months.
- **Don't**: We leverage deep synergy between SEO and ads to optimize margins. — **Do**: Connect your content strategies directly with paid campaigns so every dollar drives conversions immediately.
- **Don't**: Navigate our complex interface designed for enterprise-scale needs. — **Do**: Use a streamlined dashboard built specifically for agile growth teams who need clarity, not complexity.

## 6. Layout principles

*   **Section spacing** — Establish distinct visual chapters using `xl` (40px) between major content blocks to prevent cognitive overload and ensure users can scan dense information quickly; use `lg` (24px) for minor grouping within modules like pricing cards or feature grids.
*   **Content max width** — Constrain all text-heavy sections, particularly articles and data tables, to 650–700px centered containers to maintain perfect typographic readability across devices while leaving ample negative space on the sides to reduce visual noise.
*   **Hero structure** — Follow a strict vertical rhythm of Headline (H1) → Subcopy (brief value prop with keyword context for SEO relevance) → Primary CTA, utilizing `md` spacing between elements and full-width backgrounds only where necessary to signal premium status without distraction.
*   **Grouping / muted backgrounds** — Utilize subtle background variations like alternating off-white or 5% tinted grays (`rgba(0,0,0,0.03)`) every two screens to separate content sections naturally; apply `sm` (4px) radius cards on these backgrounds to create a "stamped" effect that emphasizes the primary action within each section.
*   **CTA prominence** — Ensure all conversion points use solid brand colors with high contrast against their specific background, rounded corners of `md` (8px) for approachability, and include subtle elevation (`sm` shadow) to lift them from the page flow; position buttons above the fold on mobile or immediately following key trust signals.
*   **Navigation patterns** — Implement a minimalist global nav that disappears below the viewport height threshold to focus attention on content consumption and lead capture forms rather than browsing depth, reserving mega-menus for complex product categories like CRM integrations where deep hierarchy exists.
*   **Footer / pricing / card patterns** — Adopt clean 2-column pricing grids that use `full` (9999px) radius pills or soft borders to denote featured tiers; structure footers as a dense yet breathable resource hub with equal-weight links, avoiding clutter by grouping tools and resources into logical semantic clusters rather than listing every tag.

This layout philosophy directly communicates Collins' identity as an authority in the professional marketing space: by prioritizing whitespace and restrained color palettes, we reduce visual friction that leads to decision paralysis—critical for high-value B2B audiences. The strict adherence to content max-widths signals respect for the reader's time and attention span, traits essential when dealing with complex topics like SEO strategy or data analytics. Furthermore, the subtle elevation effects and consistent rhythm create a sense of reliability; users who can predict how elements will interact feel secure enough to trust the platform with their marketing budgets and proprietary customer data. Ultimately, every margin is earned space that reinforces the brand's commitment to clarity over flashiness, ensuring that the content remains the undisputed star while the design quietly assures professionalism at every scroll depth.
