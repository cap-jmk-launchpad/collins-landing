# Design System: Collins
**Project ID:** ebd31ae8-0025-441d-8eea-7bbda8af377d

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
The visual identity of Collins must immediately communicate clarity and operational speed, directly countering the perception of bloated enterprise software or opaque legacy systems. As an agency brand stepping into this space, our persona reads as the **Sage** archetype: authoritative yet approachable, grounded in data but driven by wisdom rather than cold logic alone. This visual strategy avoids the aggressive tech-minimalism often found with competitors like HubSpot; instead, we embrace a sophisticated warmth that signals "professional partner" before opening the site or launching an ad campaign. The atmosphere should feel expansive and uncluttered—reflecting our core promise of eliminating friction—to instantly reassure small business owners who are wary of hidden costs and technical debt.

Narratively, Collins acts as the catalyst for sustainable growth, a concept that must be woven into every visual layer. Our design system does not merely present data; it curates insight. The brand story emphasizes replacing fragmented tools with an automated ecosystem built for speed; visually, this translates to interfaces where navigation feels effortless and hierarchy is obvious. We are positioning ourselves as the "middle ground" solution—too powerful for basic spreadsheets but simple enough for a solo marketer who needs dedicated support. This narrative anchor dictates that our visuals should feel human-centric within high-tech environments, using organic textures or softer edges to contrast against the rigid grids of traditional CRM platforms. The intent is clear: we serve the agile digital marketer and the growing brand owner who refuses to compromise between financial predictability and operational depth.

To support this positioning, four core design principles govern our visual language: **Transparency**, derived from our rejection of hidden fees; **Speed**, reflected in high-performance load times and streamlined animations; **Cohesion**, ensuring social media and CRM data looks unified rather than disjointed; and **Empowerment**, demonstrated through UI elements that invite action without intimidation. These values are not abstract concepts but visual directives—for instance, our color palette avoids the aggressive "growth-at-all-costs" reds of some competitors in favor of deep navies or calming teals that suggest stability and intelligence. Typography should be legible at a glance to reduce cognitive load for users juggling multiple channels, reinforcing the brand's commitment to replacing manual spreadsheet management with automated clarity.

Our visual tone is defined by what we choose to say versus what we leave out. In our copy and design messaging, Collins speaks in **empowering verbs** like "streamline," "automate," and "grow" rather than industry jargon like "integration points" or "API latency." We avoid the sound of a tech sales pitch that sounds impersonal; instead, we adopt the voice of an expert consultant offering solutions. Where competitors use phrases such as "enterprise-grade scale" (which implies unnecessary bloat for small teams), Collins responds with **"agile precision"** and **"seamless connections."** If a visual element risks implying complexity—such as a dense dashboard—we avoid it in favor of modular, card-based layouts that highlight **conversion rate optimization**. We do not promise to be everything for everyone; we clearly delineate our lane as the transparent alternative where every dollar spent directly fuels creativity. By adhering to this voice and atmosphere, Collins positions itself not just as software, but as a trusted partner that transforms scattered operations into an engine for business growth without the hidden costs found in established players today.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- **Background (`#ffffff`)**: The foundational canvas for all digital interfaces, ensuring high legibility and reduced eye strain during long-form reading sessions (e.g., project timelines, detailed reports). Use this as the default track for light mode; avoid applying it to small widgets where `#f4f4f5` offers a softer, less aggressive visual weight.
- **Background Muted (`#f4f4f5`)**: Designed to de-emphasize secondary sections such as metadata blocks, form field backgrounds when empty, or non-primary content areas within cards. This creates necessary hierarchy without relying solely on borders; it signals "readable but less important" information gently, aligning with the Sage archetype's calm demeanor.
- **Primary Text (`#0a0a0a`)**: The anchor for all headings (h1–h6) and primary body copy in light mode. Its near-black weight ensures maximum contrast accessibility standards are met while maintaining a professional finish. Never use this color on dark surfaces; strictly pair with `Accent On` or white text when moving to darker contexts.
- **Secondary Text (`#52525b`)**: Reserved for supporting labels, form placeholders, metadata tags (e.g., "Due Date," "Assignee"), and helper copy that guides the user without demanding attention. This gray provides a clear visual step down from primary text, guiding the eye naturally across dense data structures typical of an agency workflow tool.
- **Accent (`#485668`)**: The definitive color for high-priority actions (primary buttons), active links, and key emphasis points that drive user behavior forward in the Sage persona context. This slate-blue hue is grounded enough to command trust yet distinct enough to highlight critical paths. In hero sections or surface treatments, use this as a flat block or subtle gradient stop rather than overwhelming solid shapes; reserve for moments requiring decisive action or attention.
- **Accent On (`#fafafa`)**: Strictly the foreground color when applied over `Accent` surfaces (e.g., text on primary buttons, icons within active tabs). This near-white choice ensures excellent readability against dark backgrounds while maintaining harmony with the overall palette; avoid using black here unless customizing for specific accessibility overrides.
- **Accent Muted (`#6a727d`)**: Controls all secondary states including button hovers, pressed feedback (active), and disabled interactive elements that remain technically clickable but unactivated. This shade provides immediate visual feedback to the user while preventing the interface from feeling chaotic; use sparingly on primary CTAs if they need a de-emphasized variant for non-critical actions in multi-step forms.
- **Border (`#e4e4e7`)**: Defines separation between content blocks, input field outlines when inactive, and grid lines separating project tasks or timeline days. This light gray offers definition without visual clutter; if `Accent Muted` is too visually heavy for a specific border requirement (rare), defer to this neutral stroke to maintain the system's airy feel.

**Gradients**: While Collins generally favors flat design for stability and professional readability, gradients are employed strategically in hero banners or footer backgrounds to add depth without distraction. 
*   **Hero Patterns**: Use `Accent` as a background base fading into transparent white over 10–20px with direction moving upward-right; this subtle lift adds warmth to the opening view while keeping focus on content overlays using Primary Text colors.
*   **Surface Accents**: Apply radial gradients starting from center (lightest) transitioning outward toward `Background Muted` for large feature cards, creating a soft "glow" that suggests clarity and precision. Avoid mixing multiple gradient directions; keep them linear or gentle radial to preserve the Sage archetype's orderly nature.

**Dark-theme anchors**: The dark mode implementation preserves brand identity by maintaining exact hex values for continuity—background shifts to `#0a0a0a` (near-black), text reverts to light `#fafafa`, and accent remains anchored at `#485668`. This creates a "high contrast" inversion where the cool slate accent pops vividly against deep surfaces. Do not lighten dark accents or darken primary text; stick strictly to these anchors to prevent color bleeding or washed-out effects on OLED screens. Use subtle variations of background for sidebars (adding 0x1–2 hex steps) but never compromise the core anchor values defined here.

**Do / don't**:
*   **Don't** use `Accent` (`#485668`) as a background color unless explicitly paired with `Accent On` text; using it for decorative sidebars without clear hierarchy confuses users about interaction priority, violating the Sage's structured communication style.
*   **Do not** combine `Primary Text (#0a0a0a)` directly on top of any non-white surface in light mode (e.g., dark cards or heavy shadows); this breaks accessibility standards and forces the eye to hunt for contrast boundaries that should be handled by layout alone, creating visual friction.

**App tokens note**: All semantic roles listed above map 1:1 to CSS custom properties (`--collins-bg`, `--collins-accent-primary`, etc.) within your design token system (e.g., Tailwind or styled-system). Developers can reference these variables directly in component styles without hardcoded hex values, ensuring consistency across light/dark modes and automated theming updates.

## 3. Typography Rules
### Font Families & Strategic Rationale

**Plus Jakarta Sans (Headings & Interface Elements)**
We will deploy **Plus Jakarta Sans** exclusively for H1, H2, and H3 headings, primary CTAs, data visualizations, stats, and UI labels. This geometric humanist sans-serif is the definitive choice because its variable font architecture supports infinite weight transitions—a direct metaphor for Collins' promise of scalability without bloat. The slightly condensed yet wide x-height conveys precision and modern efficiency, aligning perfectly with the brand's positioning as an "agile" alternative to bloated legacy tools like HubSpot or Salesforce. It tells a market-facing story: fast, engineered, and devoid of unnecessary ornamentation.

**Inter (Body Copy & Microcopy)**
All paragraph text, form fields, metadata, error states, and secondary UI elements will utilize **Inter**. Inter is the industry standard for readability on screens; its distinct character set ensures that legal clauses, behavioral tracking metrics, and complex SEO data remain scannable even at small sizes. Its neutrality allows the voice of your agency—whether authoritative or empathetic—to shine through without font interference. In an ecosystem where users are fatigued by information overload from larger platforms, Inter provides a "clean air" experience that reduces cognitive load during rapid deployment workflows.

### Recommended Type Scale (Web Context)

- **H1 (Hero)**: 48 - 60px (`2.5rem`+) — `font-extrabold` to `black` — `line-height: 1.1`; `-letter-spacing`: `normal` — Anchor for value propositions; maximum impact.
- **H2 (Section)**: 36px (`~48-50px`) — `extrabold` / `bold` — `line-height: 1.2` — Section breaks and service highlights.
- **H3 (Sub-sections)**: 28 - 30px (`7rem`) — `semibold` to `bold` — `line-height: 1.3` — Feature blocks or case study headers.
- **Body Copy**: 16-18px (`~4.5rem/5rem`) — `regular` / `medium` — `leading`: `24 - 28px`; `-letter-spacing`: `0em` to `+0.02em` (for long forms) — Primary reading blocks; ensure sufficient breathing room between lines for legal/dense data.
- **Small/UI Text**: 14-15px (`~6rem`) — `medium` / `regular` — `-letter-spacing`: `normal`; Capitalization: All Caps or Title Case with reduced height scaling — Button text, price tags, meta descriptions. Do not use H3 weight for this; keep it distinct from headers via size/weight balance.
- **Captions**: 12px (`~8rem`) / Text XS — `regular` (light) to `medium` — `-letter-spacing`: `+0.04em`; Uppercase preferred if density requires clarity — Grid labels, axis text on charts.

### Pairing Rules & Weight Hierarchy

*   **The 3-Weight System:** To maintain the professional "Sage" archetype without appearing stiff, restrict your weight palette to `Light`, `Regular/Medium`, and `Bold/Extra Bold`. Avoid using `Thin` weights for body text; they reduce legibility on low-end devices common among SMBs.
*   **Contrast by Weight:** Use heavy contrast (e.g., H1 in Extra Black, Body Regular) rather than size alone to create hierarchy. This mimics the high-impact "before and after" transformations you sell.
*   **UI Consistency:** When displaying behavioral tracking stats or CRM data points within UI components, use Plus Jakarta Sans for labels if possible (maintaining visual unity with headers), but never exceed `Medium` weight in body contexts to avoid confusion between text lines.

### Tracking & Emphasis Guidelines

*   **Tight Tracking (-0.02em):** Strictly apply tight letter spacing only to full-cap lockups, data stats grids (e.g., "ROI +15%"), or H3s with `Extra Bold` weight. This creates a sense of technical precision and engineered speed. Never tighten tracking on standard body copy; it harms readability in long paragraphs regarding compliance or process descriptions.
*   **Bold for Structure:** Use bold to denote importance within text, but avoid emphasizing entire sentences unless they are the primary call-to-action (e.g., "Click here to see results"). Let italics serve exclusively for subtle nuances or quotes from case studies; overuse of italics dilutes your professional authority.
*   **Uppercase Nuance:** If using uppercase text blocks, reduce `font-size` by 4px–6px relative to the lowercase equivalent (e.g., H2 size minus 1rem) and add tight tracking (+0.03em). Do not use standard uppercase sizes for body elements; it looks amateurish and aggressive.

### Typography "Do & Don't" Checklist

**DO:**
*   **Prioritize Scannability over Decoration:** Ensure that when you display complex SEO metrics or behavioral data, the font choice (Inter) prioritizes rapid scanning over artistic flair. The user must immediately grasp the value of your automation engine without decoding dense typefaces.
*   **Maintain Consistent Baselines Across Devices:** Collins is a tool for agile teams; verify that Plus Jakarta Sans and Inter render perfectly at 1080p, tablets, and mobile widths (320px) so that growth managers can reference data on the go without visual distortion.

**DON'T:**
*   **DO NOT Use Light Italic Combinations as Headlines:** While visually elegant in a vacuum, combining `Light` weight with italics for H1/H2 headings reduces perceived authority and legibility at distance. This contradicts your positioning of strength against competitors like HubSpot or Salesforce. Always use `Bold` for headers to command attention.
*   **DO NOT Mix Font Families Within a Single Paragraph:** Resisting the temptation to swap between Plus Jakarta Sans and Inter within body copy is non-negotiable. Fragmented typography mirrors the "fragmented tools" you are solving; your brand must present a unified, cohesive ecosystem visually as well as functionally.

## 4. Component Stylings
### Component Stylings

#### Buttons & Actions
**Primary Button**: The cornerstone of conversion, utilizing `accent` (#485668) as the fill and `accent-on` (#fafafa) for foreground text/icons. Use a moderate border radius (e.g., 4px–6px) to maintain professional clarity without appearing overly rounded or playful. Label casing must be sentence case (e.g., "Get Started" rather than "GET STARTED") with medium weight typography to project confidence and readability at scale.
**Hover & Focus**: On hover, the background shifts subtly toward `accent-muted` (#6a727d) to indicate interactivity while retaining brand grounding; text opacity remains 100%. Add a clear focus ring in black or dark grey with high contrast for accessibility compliance, ensuring keyboard users never lose context.
**Secondary/Outline Button**: Utilizes the border color (`#e4e4e7`) as both stroke and fill background when clicked to indicate state change; text remains `primary-text` (#0a0a0a). This variant is reserved for non-destructive actions like "Learn More" or view options, balancing visual weight against primary calls-to-action.
**Danger/Error State**: If used, the button should adopt a muted red hue (distinct from brand accent) with white text, avoiding `accent-muted` unless specifically required to soften UI impact during critical flows.

#### Cards & Containers
**Surface Strategy**: Default card backgrounds must utilize `background-muted` (#f4f4f5), creating immediate visual separation from the pure white page body (`#ffffff`). This prevents content bleeding into adjacent sections and aids in scanning long-form information architectures typical of agency portfolios or case studies.
**Borders & Dividers**: Apply a 1px solid stroke using `border-color` (#e4e4e7). Keep these strokes light to avoid visual clutter; only increase border weight for specific section breaks or modal containers where distinct enclosure is required. Avoid heavy box-shadows entirely unless mimicking a physical document feel (drop shadow ~2px soft diffuse); prefer flat layouts with clear chromatic separation instead.
**Padding & Whitespace**: Internal padding should allow the content to breathe, typically 16px–24px for compact cards and 32px+ for feature grids or large hero containers. Ensure typography scales relative to container width—smaller text in narrow mobile cards may need reduced line-height adjustments compared to desktop equivalents.

#### Inputs & Forms
**Input Fields**: Default state uses white background with a subtle `border-color` (#e4e4e7) outline of approximately 1px–2px radius rounded corners depending on design system strictness (preferably consistent across all input types). Placeholder text should use `secondary-text` (#52525b), fading to ~50% opacity upon initial focus if needed, but preferably remaining static at 60%-80% for clarity.
**Focus Ring**: When an input is focused, replace the default browser ring with a solid black or dark grey outline (higher contrast than accent). Optionally overlay a very subtle `accent` glow in the background to signal active selection without breaking the professional aesthetic. Avoid colored rings unless they are part of error states or specific success indicators defined elsewhere in the brand system.
**Error States**: When validation fails, replace input borders with a clear red (not derived from accent palette) and prepend an icon such as exclamation mark before displaying descriptive copy below field using `secondary-text`. Remove helper text labels entirely until errors resolve to prevent confusion between guidance and error messaging.

#### Logo Lockups
**Placement Rules**: Display the Collins logo exclusively in solid black (`#0a0a0a`) or monochrome greys derived from primary/secondary palettes unless utilizing official vector assets that incorporate `accent` colors directly into typography marks like "Collins+". Ensure minimum clear space equals half-width of tallest letterform regardless of screen size.
**Variants**: For full-page headers on landing sites, use a horizontal lockup with ample left/right padding equal to font-size multiples (~0.5em). ## 5. Layout Principles
- Vertical rhythm: Establish baseline spacing units based on `md` (16px) for standard section breaks and use `xl` (40px) to `2xl` (56px) between major narrative shifts or feature zones. Maintain a consistent 8-point grid scale where possible, ensuring that paragraph margins align with `sm` (8px) while component cards utilize `lg` (16px) internal padding for breathability without visual clutter.
- Content max-width: Restrict text-heavy marketing sections to a strict `max-width: 720px` centered on the page frame to ensure legibility and reduce cognitive load, expanding only to `840px` or `960px` when presenting structured grids of product features or comparative data tables.
- Hero hierarchy rule: Implement a dominant headline that anchors left-center followed immediately by subcopy in 13–14pt for accessibility; place primary CTA buttons with generous negative space above and below to draw the eye, ensuring secondary actions are visually distinct through stroke-only styling rather than competing fills or shadows.
- Background strategy: Use muted grayscale surfaces (slightly off-white) as defaults for long-form copy sections to enhance readability of dark text, reserving pure white backgrounds strictly for modular component areas like feature lists; shift to accent-tinted gradients or soft solid colors only in Hero headers and footer zones where full visual impact is required.
- Grid alignment: Apply 8px increments across all layout structures, enforcing strict left-alignment for marketing copy while utilizing flexbox with `gap` properties equal to half the base spacing unit for component grids; ensure product UI elements maintain consistent horizontal baselines regardless of variable content height in adjacent columns.
- Implementation map: Enforce these rhythm and width rules on global landing pages (`/`, `/about`), detailed feature documentation hubs, client dashboard home views (`/dashboard/home`), and settings configuration screens where clarity prevents user error; apply component-specific elevation shadows consistently across all interactive forms and modal dialogs within the application shell.
- Alignment status: **Typography** — Limit font weight choices to three primary tiers (Regular/Medium/Semi-Bold) strictly for headlines versus body copy, avoiding dense text blocks that exceed 2 lines of semi-bold or bold weights; **Color usage** — Assign brand accent colors exclusively to interactive elements like buttons and hyperlinks while keeping informational data in neutral grays to maintain the "transparent" trust signal promised by Collins; **Spacing zones** — Reserve `xl` (40px) gaps solely for separating distinct functional modules, utilizing smaller units within those blocks to create a contained sense of order without feeling boxed-in; **Component elevation** — Apply soft shadow variations only when cards float above base layers, removing shadows entirely from inline elements or content sitting directly on the page background.
- Do / don't: **Do** use ample whitespace around high-value insights and pricing tables to signal importance; **Don't crowd adjacent informational panels with too many visual noise indicators**. **Do** prioritize left-aligned text blocks that allow readers easy scanning across columns of metrics; **Don't force right-alignment on long-form copy which disrupts natural reading flow for English-speaking users.

## 6. Spacing & Radius Scale
Space defines the rhythm of Collins' visual language; it dictates how dense information feels to your target enterprise clients and establishes an air of sophisticated order that separates you from commodity service providers. Our token scale moves strictly in multiples of 4, ensuring a grid-based precision suitable for complex data visualizations or high-density pitch decks.

**Full Spacing Scale with Usage Guidance**
Use these tokens to control horizontal rhythm across all deliverables:

*   **`--space-xs (4px)`**: Reserved exclusively for micro-interactions and dense alignment tasks within UI components, such as icon-to-text padding inside a feature list or the gap between stacked status badges. Do not use this token as section margin; it creates a claustrophobic feel on desktop screens where you need to breathe room around your value propositions.
*   **`--space-sm (8px)`**: The standard for single-line text elements and tight grouping of related items, like the gap between a label input in an analytics form or spacing between two adjacent column headers. It signals "these are connected concepts" without breaking flow. Avoid using `sm` between paragraph blocks; it visually disconnects sentences that belong together conceptually.
*   **`--space-md (16px)`**: The workhorse of the system, used for single-block padding on interactive elements and margins between list items or inline media. This is your go-to token for mobile viewports where screen real estate is at a premium but clarity remains paramount. Do not use `md` as outer container margin; it will cause layout collision with adjacent content blocks in 4k presentations.
*   **`--space-lg (24px)`**: Defines the separation between distinct semantic sections on A4 or widescreen layouts, such as space above a client case study header or margins flanking a hero image grid. This token creates a "breathable" look essential for high-end B2B branding. Never use `lg` inside a card body; it dilutes the density of your data visualization and makes the interface feel loose rather than controlled.
*   **`--space-xl (40px)`**: Reserved for major section dividers, such as the space before/after primary call-to-action banners or between case study summaries. It commands attention by creating a dramatic pause in reading rhythm, guiding the user's eye to high-priority content like "Why Us" sections. Avoid overusing `xl` on mobile; it will push secondary navigation elements off-screen.
*   **`--space-2xl (56px)`**: The architectural spine of long-form reports and landing pages, used for top-level section breaks or the gap between a main hero banner and its sub-narrative text. This token establishes hierarchy by enforcing significant visual distance; use only where you need to reset user context before introducing new value pillars.

**Radius Scale & Component Mapping**
Collins treats corners as indicators of interaction mode: sharper edges for functional tools, softer curves for human-centric marketing assets. All components strictly map to these four tokens based on their semantic weight in the project.

- **`--radius-sm`**: 4px — Input fields, table headers, small data cards, alert banners. — Avoid for buttons; it looks too utilitarian and industrial unless part of a "raw" style module.
- **`--radius-md`**: 8px — Standard buttons, dropdown menus, standard card containers, modal dialogs. — Not appropriate for hero sections or organic shapes (images); strictly rectangular bounds only.
- **`--radius-lg`**: 12px — Large feature cards, floating action buttons (FABs), navigation pills within dashboards. — Use sparingly on mobile to prevent the interface from looking "squishy" when content expands vertically.
- **`--radius-full`**: 9999px — Tags/ Pills that wrap long text ("Enterprise", "B2B"), pill-shaped menu buttons, notification badges. — Never apply this to full-page containers or hero backgrounds; it forces awkward cropping of images and breaks the page layout geometry.

**Elevation System**
Depth in Collins is achieved via subtle shadows rather than complex layering. These tokens must be applied consistently to maintain a sense of weightless professionalism:
*   **`--elevation-sm`**: `0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)` — Used for active states in toolbars or slightly raised input fields where depth should feel negligible but present.
*   **`--elevation-md`**: `0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)` — The standard drop shadow for cards and modals resting on a neutral background. Use this when separating content blocks without drawing heavy borders.
*   **`--elevation-lg`**: `0 12px 32px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)` — Reserved for "floating" elements like primary CTAs in hero sections or notification popups that demand immediate visual hierarchy over background content.

**Common Pattern Mappings**
Apply these presets to ensure your output remains cohesive across different client needs:
*   **Button Patterns**: Text buttons use `padding: var(--space-xs) var(--space-md)` with no border radius (flat utility). Icon-only action buttons utilize `--radius-lg` for approachability.
